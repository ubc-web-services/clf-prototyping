A New Commons for the Science Precinct

   

In November, the residents of Totem Park and Place Vanier pulled out all the stops — and a lot of plugs — in an intense bid to save energy and water, as part of UBC’s third annual Do It In The Dark competition.

Candle making, glow-in-the-dark sports, paper recycling, cold-water laundry lessons — the residents of Totem Park and Place Vanier had a lot of activities to choose from this November. For three weeks, the more than three thousand students who live in UBC’s two largest residence complexes were competing in “ Do It in the Dark” (DIITD), an energy and water conservation competition. Hosted by Lucid Design, the competition challenged students to find innovative ways to save energy and water in their residence buildings.

In 2010 Totem Park participated in the first version of “Do It In the Dark,” as the only Canadian entrant in a North American competition called the Campus Conservation Nationals. For the last two years the residence, home to more than 1,750 students, has competed alongside (and sometimes against) Place Vanier, which houses another 1,370 students.
